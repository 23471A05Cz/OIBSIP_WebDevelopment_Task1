A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

“You have to dream before your dreams can come true. Thinking is the capital, ideas are the wealth, and hard work is the key to success. Believe in yourself, work with dedication, and never fear failure, because failure is just a stepping stone to greatness.”

Biography

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was one of India's most respected scientists and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background but achieved great success through dedication and hard work. He played a key role in India's missile development programs and became known as the "Missile Man of India." His contributions to organizations like ISRO and DRDO helped strengthen India's defense and space research. Dr. Kalam was also deeply passionate about education and inspiring young people. Through his speeches, books, and interactions with students, he encouraged millions to dream big and work for the progress of the nation.
